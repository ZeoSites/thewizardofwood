Common Epoxy Flooring Issues and Solutions

There's no denying the durability of epoxy flooring, but the fact that problems may arise with time is inevitable. Here is a list of common epoxy flooring issues and their possible solutions:

Cracking:

Excessive weight, temperature fluctuations, and high-traffic can result in epoxy floor cracking. To minimize the risk, ensure stable substrate before installation. If cracking has occurred, fill them with epoxy crack filler.

Blistering:

Trap air bubbles or moisture often cause blisters to form during or after the installation of epoxy flooring. To avoid this, ensure adequately prepare the surface with careful cleaning and thorough drying. If it appears over time, the affected part can be removed, and new epoxy can be reapplied on the surface.

Peeling:

This is another issue that can arise if the surface was not properly prepared before installation. Again, ensure surface preparation is done correctly for a proper bonding surface. Also, make certain to follow the prescribed instructions for mixing and applying the epoxy.

Discoloration:

Epoxy floors may experience discoloration over time because of UV light exposure. To avoid this, endeavor to apply UV-protective topcoat to shield the floor against UV exposure. Also, conduct regular and appropriate cleaning to minimize discoloration of your epoxy floor.

Scratches:

Your epoxy floors might experience scratches caused by sharp objects or heavy furniture. You can prevent this by using pads for your furniture. Also, sweep and clean the floor regularly to get rid of sharp items and debris that could lead to scratches.

FAQs

How much does epoxy cost per foot?

Epoxy flooring costs can be estimated at as low as $2.50 per square foot when acquiring a significant quantity for extensive coverage. For a smaller-scale job, you’re typically only expecting to pay around $8 per square foot for high-quality epoxy.

Is epoxy floor cheaper than tiles?

Epoxy flooring is always cheaper than tiled flooring, although this is on the quality and finishing of what you compare. Epoxy floor costs approximately $30-$100 per sqm installed. The price range for vinyl tiles is similar, while for ceramic tiles the average cost is a lot more than $90-$150 per square meter.
