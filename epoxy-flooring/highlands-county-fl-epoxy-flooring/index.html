Common Epoxy Flooring Issues and Solutions

It is indisputable that epoxy flooring are durable, but the fact that it can generate issues over time can not be helped. Here is an overview of common problems with epoxy flooring and how to address them:

Cracking:

Weight overload, temperature changes, and heavy traffic can result in epoxy floor cracking. To minimize the risk, ensure the substrate is stable before installing the epoxy flooring. If cracking has occurred, repair them with epoxy crack filler.

Blistering:

Trap air or moisture often cause blisters to form during or after the installation of epoxy flooring. To avoid this, ensure your surface is properly prepared with meticulous cleaning and drying. If blisters become visible over time, the affected part can be removed, and new epoxy can be installed on the affected area.

Peeling:

This is another challenge that can surface if the surface was not properly prepared before installation. Again, ensure surface preparation is done correctly for establishing a suitable bonding surface. Also, ensure to follow the laid down instructions for mixing and application.

Discoloration:

Discoloration may occur in epoxy floors with the passage of time as a result of UV light exposure. To mitigate this, endeavor to coat the surface with a UV-protective topcoat to safeguard the floor from UV exposure. Also, conduct regular and appropriate cleaning to minimize discoloration of your epoxy floor.

Scratches:

Scratches may develop on your epoxy floors due to sharp objects or heavy furniture. Prevent this by placing furniture pads. Also, sweep and clean the floor regularly to remove sharp objects and other debris that can cause scratches.

FAQs

How much does epoxy cost per foot?

You can estimate epoxy flooring expenses as low as $2.50 per square foot when you’re buying in bulk to cover several thousand square feet. For a smaller-scale job, you’re typically only looking at $8 per square foot on the higher end.

Is epoxy floor cheaper than tiles?

In most cases, epoxy flooring is more budget-friendly than tiled flooring, although this is on the quality and finishing of what you compare. Epoxy floor costs approximately $30-$100 per sqm installed. The price range for vinyl tiles is similar, while for ceramic tiles the average cost is a lot more than $90-$150 per square meter.
