Common Epoxy Flooring Issues and Solutions

There's no denying the durability of epoxy flooring, but the fact that problems may arise with time is inevitable. Here is a list of common epoxy flooring issues and their possible solutions:

Cracking:

Weight overload, temperature changes, and heavy traffic can result in epoxy floor cracking. To prevent this, ensure the substrate is stable before installing the epoxy flooring. If they had already happened, fill them with epoxy crack filler.

Blistering:

Trapped air or moisture often cause blisters to form during or after installation of the epoxy flooring. To minimize the risk of this happening, ensure adequately prepare the surface with meticulous cleaning and drying. If blisters become visible over time, the affected part can be removed, and new epoxy can be reapplied on the surface.

Peeling:

This is another challenge that can surface if the surface was not properly prepared before installation. Again, ensure surface preparation is done correctly for a proper bonding surface. Also, make certain to follow the prescribed instructions for mixing and applying the epoxy.

Discoloration:

Discoloration may occur in epoxy floors with the passage of time because of UV light exposure. To mitigate this, endeavor to apply UV-protective topcoat to safeguard the floor from UV exposure. Also, ensure proper and regular cleaning to lessen the likelihood of discoloration on your epoxy floor.

Scratches:

Your epoxy floors might experience scratches caused by sharp objects or heavy furniture. You can prevent this by using pads for your furniture. Also, regularly sweep and clean the floor to get rid of sharp items and debris that could lead to scratches.

FAQs

How much does epoxy cost per foot?

Epoxy flooring costs can be estimated at as low as $2.50 per square foot when acquiring a significant quantity for extensive coverage. For smaller-scale applications, you’re typically only expecting to pay around $8 per square foot for high-quality epoxy.

Is epoxy floor cheaper than tiles?

Epoxy flooring is always cheaper than tiled flooring, although this is subject to the quality and finishing standards being compared. Epoxy floor costs approximately $30-$100 per sqm installed. The price range for vinyl tiles is similar, while for ceramic tiles the average cost is a lot more than $90-$150 per square meter.
