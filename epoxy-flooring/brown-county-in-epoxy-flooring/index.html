Common Epoxy Flooring Issues and Solutions

There's no denying the durability of epoxy flooring, but the fact that problems may arise with time is inevitable. Here is a list of common epoxy flooring issues and their possible solutions:

Cracking:

Excessive weight, temperature fluctuations, and high-traffic can cause epoxy floors to crack. To prevent this, ensure stable substrate before installation. If they had already happened, fill them with epoxy crack filler.

Blistering:

Trapped air bubbles or moisture often cause blisters to form during or after installation of the epoxy flooring. To minimize the risk of this happening, ensure your surface is properly prepared with careful cleaning and thorough drying. If blisters become visible over time, the affected part can be removed, and new epoxy can be installed on the affected area.

Peeling:

This is another challenge that can surface when the surface was not properly prepped prior to installation. Again, ensure surface preparation is done correctly for establishing a suitable bonding surface. Also, make certain to follow the prescribed instructions for mixing and applying the epoxy.

Discoloration:

Discoloration may occur in epoxy floors with the passage of time as a result of UV light exposure. To mitigate this, endeavor to apply UV-protective topcoat to safeguard the floor from UV exposure. Also, ensure proper and regular cleaning to minimize discoloration of your epoxy floor.

Scratches:

Scratches may develop on your epoxy floors due to sharp objects or heavy furniture. You can prevent this by using pads for your furniture. Also, regularly sweep and clean the floor to get rid of sharp items and debris that could lead to scratches.

How much does epoxy cost per foot?

Epoxy flooring costs can be estimated at as low as $2.50 per square foot when acquiring a significant quantity for extensive coverage. For smaller-scale applications, you’re typically only looking at $8 per square foot on the higher end.

Is epoxy floor cheaper than tiles?

Epoxy flooring is always cheaper than tiled flooring, although this is on the quality and finishing of what you compare. Epoxy floor costs approximately $30-$100 per sqm installed. The price range for vinyl tiles is similar, while for ceramic tiles the average cost is a lot more than $90-$150 per square meter.
