Common Epoxy Flooring Issues and Solutions

There's no denying the durability of epoxy flooring, but the fact that it can generate issues over time can not be helped. Here is a list of common epoxy flooring issues and their possible solutions:

Cracking:

Weight overload, temperature changes, and heavy traffic can result in epoxy floor cracking. To minimize the risk, ensure the substrate is stable before installing the epoxy flooring. If cracking has occurred, fill them with epoxy crack filler.

Blistering:

Trapped air bubbles or moisture frequently result in the development of blisters during or after installation of the epoxy flooring. To avoid this, ensure your surface is properly prepared with meticulous cleaning and drying. If it appears over time, the affected part can be removed, and new epoxy can be reapplied on the surface.

Peeling:

This is another issue that can arise if the surface was not properly prepared before installation. Again, ensure surfaces are prepared properly for a proper bonding surface. Also, ensure to follow the laid down instructions for mixing and application.

Discoloration:

Discoloration may occur in epoxy floors with the passage of time as a result of UV light exposure. To avoid this, endeavor to apply UV-protective topcoat to shield the floor against UV exposure. Also, ensure proper and regular cleaning to lessen the likelihood of discoloration on your epoxy floor.

Scratches:

Your epoxy floors might experience scratches due to sharp objects or heavy furniture. Prevent this by placing furniture pads. Also, sweep and clean the floor regularly to get rid of sharp items and debris that could lead to scratches.

How much does epoxy cost per foot?

You can estimate epoxy flooring expenses as low as $2.50 per square foot when you’re buying in bulk to cover several thousand square feet. For a smaller-scale job, you’re typically only expecting to pay around $8 per square foot for high-quality epoxy.

Is epoxy floor cheaper than tiles?

In most cases, epoxy flooring is more budget-friendly than tiled flooring, although this is on the quality and finishing of what you compare. Epoxy floor costs approximately $30-$100 per sqm installed. The price range for vinyl tiles is similar, while for ceramic tiles you can expect an average cost significantly higher, typically ranging from $90 to $150 per square meter.
