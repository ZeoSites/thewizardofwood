Common Epoxy Flooring Issues and Solutions

It is indisputable that epoxy flooring are durable, but the fact that it can generate issues over time can not be helped. Here is an overview of common problems with epoxy flooring and how to address them:

Cracking:

Excessive weight, temperature fluctuations, and high-traffic can result in epoxy floor cracking. To prevent this, ensure the substrate is stable before installing the epoxy flooring. If cracking has occurred, fill them with epoxy crack filler.

Blistering:

Trapped air bubbles or moisture frequently result in the development of blisters during or after installation of the epoxy flooring. To minimize the risk of this happening, ensure adequately prepare the surface with meticulous cleaning and drying. If blisters become visible over time, the affected part can be removed, and new epoxy can be reapplied on the surface.

Peeling:

This is another challenge that can surface when the surface was not properly prepped prior to installation. Again, ensure surfaces are prepared properly for a proper bonding surface. Also, ensure to follow the laid down instructions for mixing and application.

Discoloration:

Discoloration may occur in epoxy floors with the passage of time because of UV light exposure. To avoid this, endeavor to coat the surface with a UV-protective topcoat to shield the floor against UV exposure. Also, ensure proper and regular cleaning to minimize discoloration of your epoxy floor.

Scratches:

Scratches may develop on your epoxy floors caused by sharp objects or heavy furniture. You can prevent this by using pads for your furniture. Also, regularly sweep and clean the floor to remove sharp objects and other debris that can cause scratches.

FAQs

How much does epoxy cost per foot?

You can estimate epoxy flooring expenses as low as $2.50 per square foot when acquiring a significant quantity for extensive coverage. For smaller-scale applications, you’re typically only expecting to pay around $8 per square foot for high-quality epoxy.

Is epoxy floor cheaper than tiles?

Epoxy flooring is always cheaper than tiled flooring, although this is on the quality and finishing of what you compare. Epoxy floor costs approximately $30-$100 per sqm installed. The price range for vinyl tiles is similar, while for ceramic tiles you can expect an average cost significantly higher, typically ranging from $90 to $150 per square meter.
