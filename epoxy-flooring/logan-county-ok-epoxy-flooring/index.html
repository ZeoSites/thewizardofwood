Common Epoxy Flooring Issues and Solutions

It is indisputable that epoxy flooring are durable, but the fact that it can generate issues over time can not be helped. Here is a list of common epoxy flooring issues and their possible solutions:

Cracking:

Excessive weight, temperature fluctuations, and high-traffic can result in epoxy floor cracking. To prevent this, ensure stable substrate before installation. If they had already happened, fill them with epoxy crack filler.

Blistering:

Trapped air or moisture often cause blisters to form during or after the installation of epoxy flooring. To avoid this, ensure your surface is properly prepared with careful cleaning and thorough drying. If it appears over time, the affected part can be removed, and new epoxy can be reapplied on the surface.

Peeling:

This is another issue that can arise when the surface was not properly prepped prior to installation. Again, ensure surfaces are prepared properly for a proper bonding surface. Also, ensure to follow the laid down instructions for mixing and application.

Discoloration:

Discoloration may occur in epoxy floors with the passage of time as a result of UV light exposure. To avoid this, endeavor to coat the surface with a UV-protective topcoat to shield the floor against UV exposure. Also, conduct regular and appropriate cleaning to lessen the likelihood of discoloration on your epoxy floor.

Scratches:

Scratches may develop on your epoxy floors due to sharp objects or heavy furniture. You can prevent this by using pads for your furniture. Also, regularly sweep and clean the floor to remove sharp objects and other debris that can cause scratches.

FAQs

How much does epoxy cost per foot?

Epoxy flooring costs can be estimated at as low as $2.50 per square foot when you’re buying in bulk to cover several thousand square feet. For a smaller-scale job, you’re typically only expecting to pay around $8 per square foot for high-quality epoxy.

Is epoxy floor cheaper than tiles?

In most cases, epoxy flooring is more budget-friendly than tiled flooring, although this is on the quality and finishing of what you compare. Epoxy floor costs approximately $30-$100 per sqm installed. The price range for vinyl tiles is similar, while for ceramic tiles the average cost is a lot more than $90-$150 per square meter.
