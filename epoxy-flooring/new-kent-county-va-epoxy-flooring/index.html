Common Epoxy Flooring Issues and Solutions

It is indisputable that epoxy flooring are durable, but the fact that it can generate issues over time can not be helped. Here is a list of common epoxy flooring issues and their possible solutions:

Cracking:

Excessive weight, temperature fluctuations, and high-traffic can cause epoxy floors to crack. To minimize the risk, ensure the substrate is stable before installing the epoxy flooring. If cracking has occurred, repair them with epoxy crack filler.

Blistering:

Trap air bubbles or moisture frequently result in the development of blisters during or after the installation of epoxy flooring. To avoid this, ensure adequately prepare the surface with meticulous cleaning and drying. If blisters become visible over time, the affected part can be removed, and new epoxy can be installed on the affected area.

Peeling:

This is another challenge that can surface when the surface was not properly prepped prior to installation. Again, ensure surface preparation is done correctly for a proper bonding surface. Also, make certain to follow the prescribed instructions for mixing and applying the epoxy.

Discoloration:

Epoxy floors may experience discoloration over time as a result of UV light exposure. To mitigate this, endeavor to coat the surface with a UV-protective topcoat to shield the floor against UV exposure. Also, ensure proper and regular cleaning to lessen the likelihood of discoloration on your epoxy floor.

Scratches:

Your epoxy floors might experience scratches caused by sharp objects or heavy furniture. You can prevent this by using pads for your furniture. Also, sweep and clean the floor regularly to remove sharp objects and other debris that can cause scratches.

FAQs

How much does epoxy cost per foot?

Epoxy flooring costs can be estimated at as low as $2.50 per square foot when you’re buying in bulk to cover several thousand square feet. For a smaller-scale job, you’re typically only expecting to pay around $8 per square foot for high-quality epoxy.

Is epoxy floor cheaper than tiles?

Epoxy flooring is always cheaper than tiled flooring, although this is on the quality and finishing of what you compare. Epoxy floor costs approximately $30-$100 per sqm installed. The price range for vinyl tiles is similar, while for ceramic tiles you can expect an average cost significantly higher, typically ranging from $90 to $150 per square meter.
