Common Epoxy Flooring Issues and Solutions

There's no denying the durability of epoxy flooring, but the fact that problems may arise with time is inevitable. Here is a list of common epoxy flooring issues and their possible solutions:

Cracking:

Excessive weight, temperature fluctuations, and high-traffic can result in epoxy floor cracking. To minimize the risk, ensure stable substrate before installation. If they had already happened, repair them with epoxy crack filler.

Blistering:

Trapped air bubbles or moisture frequently result in the development of blisters during or after the installation of epoxy flooring. To avoid this, ensure your surface is properly prepared with meticulous cleaning and drying. If it appears over time, the affected part can be removed, and new epoxy can be installed on the affected area.

Peeling:

This is another issue that can arise when the surface was not properly prepped prior to installation. Again, ensure surfaces are prepared properly for establishing a suitable bonding surface. Also, ensure to follow the laid down instructions for mixing and application.

Discoloration:

Discoloration may occur in epoxy floors with the passage of time as a result of UV light exposure. To avoid this, endeavor to coat the surface with a UV-protective topcoat to shield the floor against UV exposure. Also, ensure proper and regular cleaning to minimize discoloration of your epoxy floor.

Scratches:

Your epoxy floors might experience scratches due to sharp objects or heavy furniture. Prevent this by placing furniture pads. Also, sweep and clean the floor regularly to get rid of sharp items and debris that could lead to scratches.

FAQs

How much does epoxy cost per foot?

You can estimate epoxy flooring expenses as low as $2.50 per square foot when you’re buying in bulk to cover several thousand square feet. For smaller-scale applications, you’re typically only looking at $8 per square foot on the higher end.

Is epoxy floor cheaper than tiles?

Epoxy flooring is always cheaper than tiled flooring, although this is subject to the quality and finishing standards being compared. Epoxy floor costs approximately $30-$100 per sqm installed. The price range for vinyl tiles is similar, while for ceramic tiles the average cost is a lot more than $90-$150 per square meter.
