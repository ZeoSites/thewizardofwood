Common Epoxy Flooring Issues and Solutions

There's no denying the durability of epoxy flooring, but the fact that it can generate issues over time can not be helped. Here is a list of common epoxy flooring issues and their possible solutions:

Cracking:

Excessive weight, temperature fluctuations, and high-traffic can result in epoxy floor cracking. To prevent this, ensure the substrate is stable before installing the epoxy flooring. If cracking has occurred, fill them with epoxy crack filler.

Blistering:

Trapped air bubbles or moisture frequently result in the development of blisters during or after installation of the epoxy flooring. To avoid this, ensure your surface is properly prepared with careful cleaning and thorough drying. If it appears over time, the affected part can be removed, and new epoxy can be reapplied on the surface.

Peeling:

This is another challenge that can surface when the surface was not properly prepped prior to installation. Again, ensure surfaces are prepared properly for establishing a suitable bonding surface. Also, ensure to follow the laid down instructions for mixing and application.

Discoloration:

Discoloration may occur in epoxy floors with the passage of time as a result of UV light exposure. To mitigate this, endeavor to coat the surface with a UV-protective topcoat to safeguard the floor from UV exposure. Also, conduct regular and appropriate cleaning to minimize discoloration of your epoxy floor.

Scratches:

Scratches may develop on your epoxy floors due to sharp objects or heavy furniture. You can prevent this by using pads for your furniture. Also, sweep and clean the floor regularly to get rid of sharp items and debris that could lead to scratches.

FAQs

How much does epoxy cost per foot?

Epoxy flooring costs can be estimated at as low as $2.50 per square foot when you’re buying in bulk to cover several thousand square feet. For a smaller-scale job, you’re typically only looking at $8 per square foot on the higher end.

Is epoxy floor cheaper than tiles?

In most cases, epoxy flooring is more budget-friendly than tiled flooring, although this is on the quality and finishing of what you compare. Epoxy floor costs approximately $30-$100 per sqm installed. The price range for vinyl tiles is similar, while for ceramic tiles you can expect an average cost significantly higher, typically ranging from $90 to $150 per square meter.
