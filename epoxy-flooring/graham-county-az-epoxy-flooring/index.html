Common Epoxy Flooring Issues and Solutions

It is indisputable that epoxy flooring are durable, but the fact that it can generate issues over time can not be helped. Here is a list of common epoxy flooring issues and their possible solutions:

Cracking:

Weight overload, temperature changes, and heavy traffic can result in epoxy floor cracking. To prevent this, ensure stable substrate before installation. If cracking has occurred, fill them with epoxy crack filler.

Blistering:

Trap air bubbles or moisture often cause blisters to form during or after installation of the epoxy flooring. To avoid this, ensure adequately prepare the surface with careful cleaning and thorough drying. If blisters become visible over time, the affected part can be removed, and new epoxy can be installed on the affected area.

Peeling:

This is another challenge that can surface when the surface was not properly prepped prior to installation. Again, ensure surfaces are prepared properly for a proper bonding surface. Also, ensure to follow the laid down instructions for mixing and application.

Discoloration:

Epoxy floors may experience discoloration over time because of UV light exposure. To mitigate this, endeavor to coat the surface with a UV-protective topcoat to shield the floor against UV exposure. Also, ensure proper and regular cleaning to lessen the likelihood of discoloration on your epoxy floor.

Scratches:

Scratches may develop on your epoxy floors due to sharp objects or heavy furniture. You can prevent this by using pads for your furniture. Also, sweep and clean the floor regularly to get rid of sharp items and debris that could lead to scratches.

FAQs

How much does epoxy cost per foot?

You can estimate epoxy flooring expenses as low as $2.50 per square foot when you’re buying in bulk to cover several thousand square feet. For a smaller-scale job, you’re typically only expecting to pay around $8 per square foot for high-quality epoxy.

Is epoxy floor cheaper than tiles?

In most cases, epoxy flooring is more budget-friendly than tiled flooring, although this is on the quality and finishing of what you compare. Epoxy floor costs approximately $30-$100 per sqm installed. The price range for vinyl tiles is similar, while for ceramic tiles you can expect an average cost significantly higher, typically ranging from $90 to $150 per square meter.

Is it cheaper to epoxy the floor?

Indeed! If you've ever wished for colorful flooring but hesitated due to maintenance costs, epoxy might be the perfect choice for you! You don’t have to be concerned about staining as epoxy flooring is resistant to oil, gas, bleach, and most chemicals and materials.
