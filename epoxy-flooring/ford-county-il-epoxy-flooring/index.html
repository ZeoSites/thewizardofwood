Common Epoxy Flooring Issues and Solutions

It is indisputable that epoxy flooring are durable, but the fact that problems may arise with time is inevitable. Here is an overview of common problems with epoxy flooring and how to address them:

Cracking:

Excessive weight, temperature fluctuations, and high-traffic can cause epoxy floors to crack. To minimize the risk, ensure stable substrate before installation. If they had already happened, fill them with epoxy crack filler.

Blistering:

Trap air bubbles or moisture often cause blisters to form during or after installation of the epoxy flooring. To avoid this, ensure your surface is properly prepared with careful cleaning and thorough drying. If blisters become visible over time, the affected part can be removed, and new epoxy can be reapplied on the surface.

Peeling:

This is another issue that can arise when the surface was not properly prepped prior to installation. Again, ensure surfaces are prepared properly for establishing a suitable bonding surface. Also, make certain to follow the prescribed instructions for mixing and applying the epoxy.

Discoloration:

Discoloration may occur in epoxy floors with the passage of time as a result of UV light exposure. To mitigate this, endeavor to apply UV-protective topcoat to safeguard the floor from UV exposure. Also, conduct regular and appropriate cleaning to minimize discoloration of your epoxy floor.

Scratches:

Scratches may develop on your epoxy floors due to sharp objects or heavy furniture. You can prevent this by using pads for your furniture. Also, regularly sweep and clean the floor to get rid of sharp items and debris that could lead to scratches.

FAQs

How much does epoxy cost per foot?

Epoxy flooring costs can be estimated at as low as $2.50 per square foot when acquiring a significant quantity for extensive coverage. For a smaller-scale job, you’re typically only looking at $8 per square foot on the higher end.

Is epoxy floor cheaper than tiles?

In most cases, epoxy flooring is more budget-friendly than tiled flooring, although this is subject to the quality and finishing standards being compared. Epoxy floor costs approximately $30-$100 per sqm installed. The price range for vinyl tiles is similar, while for ceramic tiles you can expect an average cost significantly higher, typically ranging from $90 to $150 per square meter.
