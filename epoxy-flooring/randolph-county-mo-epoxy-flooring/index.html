Common Epoxy Flooring Issues and Solutions

It is indisputable that epoxy flooring are durable, but the fact that problems may arise with time is inevitable. Here is an overview of common problems with epoxy flooring and how to address them:

Cracking:

Excessive weight, temperature fluctuations, and high-traffic can cause epoxy floors to crack. To minimize the risk, ensure the substrate is stable before installing the epoxy flooring. If cracking has occurred, repair them with epoxy crack filler.

Blistering:

Trap air or moisture frequently result in the development of blisters during or after installation of the epoxy flooring. To minimize the risk of this happening, ensure adequately prepare the surface with meticulous cleaning and drying. If blisters become visible over time, the affected part can be removed, and new epoxy can be installed on the affected area.

Peeling:

This is another issue that can arise when the surface was not properly prepped prior to installation. Again, ensure surfaces are prepared properly for establishing a suitable bonding surface. Also, make certain to follow the prescribed instructions for mixing and applying the epoxy.

Discoloration:

Discoloration may occur in epoxy floors with the passage of time as a result of UV light exposure. To mitigate this, endeavor to apply UV-protective topcoat to shield the floor against UV exposure. Also, conduct regular and appropriate cleaning to lessen the likelihood of discoloration on your epoxy floor.

Scratches:

Scratches may develop on your epoxy floors caused by sharp objects or heavy furniture. You can prevent this by using pads for your furniture. Also, regularly sweep and clean the floor to remove sharp objects and other debris that can cause scratches.

How much does epoxy cost per foot?

Epoxy flooring costs can be estimated at as low as $2.50 per square foot when acquiring a significant quantity for extensive coverage. For a smaller-scale job, you’re typically only looking at $8 per square foot on the higher end.

Is epoxy floor cheaper than tiles?

Epoxy flooring is always cheaper than tiled flooring, although this is on the quality and finishing of what you compare. Epoxy floor costs approximately $30-$100 per sqm installed. The price range for vinyl tiles is similar, while for ceramic tiles the average cost is a lot more than $90-$150 per square meter.
