Common Epoxy Flooring Issues and Solutions

It is indisputable that epoxy flooring are durable, but the fact that problems may arise with time is inevitable. Here is an overview of common problems with epoxy flooring and how to address them:

Cracking:

Excessive weight, temperature fluctuations, and high-traffic can cause epoxy floors to crack. To minimize the risk, ensure stable substrate before installation. If they had already happened, fill them with epoxy crack filler.

Blistering:

Trapped air or moisture frequently result in the development of blisters during or after the installation of epoxy flooring. To avoid this, ensure adequately prepare the surface with careful cleaning and thorough drying. If it appears over time, the affected part can be removed, and new epoxy can be reapplied on the surface.

Peeling:

This is another issue that can arise if the surface was not properly prepared before installation. Again, ensure surface preparation is done correctly for establishing a suitable bonding surface. Also, ensure to follow the laid down instructions for mixing and application.

Discoloration:

Discoloration may occur in epoxy floors with the passage of time because of UV light exposure. To mitigate this, endeavor to apply UV-protective topcoat to safeguard the floor from UV exposure. Also, conduct regular and appropriate cleaning to minimize discoloration of your epoxy floor.

Scratches:

Your epoxy floors might experience scratches caused by sharp objects or heavy furniture. Prevent this by placing furniture pads. Also, sweep and clean the floor regularly to remove sharp objects and other debris that can cause scratches.

FAQs

How much does epoxy cost per foot?

Epoxy flooring costs can be estimated at as low as $2.50 per square foot when you’re buying in bulk to cover several thousand square feet. For smaller-scale applications, you’re typically only looking at $8 per square foot on the higher end.

Is epoxy floor cheaper than tiles?

Epoxy flooring is always cheaper than tiled flooring, although this is on the quality and finishing of what you compare. Epoxy floor costs approximately $30-$100 per sqm installed. The price range for vinyl tiles is similar, while for ceramic tiles you can expect an average cost significantly higher, typically ranging from $90 to $150 per square meter.

Is it cheaper to epoxy the floor?

Yes! Have you ever wanted to have floorings with bright colors but always considered the cost of maintenance, epoxy may be your answer! You don’t have to be concerned about staining as epoxy flooring is resistant to oil, gas, bleach, and most chemicals and materials.
